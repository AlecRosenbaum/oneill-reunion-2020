Accomodations

We will be staying at this lovely house on Lake Harmony! It has 14 bedrooms and 12 baths and sleeps 42 so there should be more than enough room for all of us! There are two full kitchens, three decks, and two small docks!

The ten single bedrooms will be offered to the parental units first and any leftovers will be on a first come first serve basis. The remaining four bedrooms have four bunk beds, we can set up a chart for accomodations.

Because it is such a large house, they ask that we provide our own linens, so please bring a set of sheets and towels :)

There's ample parking, but only 9 spots actually in front of the house. There's a small lot about a mile away we can also store cars at, but we'd need to coordinate trips back and forth unless you're fine with walking.

Cost Breakdown

Because of Grandpa’s generous help with planning this reunion, the cost that we need to cover is about 5k. This amount covers the remainder of the house and also a food budget. Alec and I will be purchasing food for the house with the remaining funds. We plan to buy enough food to at least cover breakfast and lunch for everyone for each day. We're also planning to get reunion T-shirts!

Costs for the reunion will obviously fluctuate with the number of people who actually plan on attending. For 35 people, the cost is only $150 per person for four days and three nights.

Costs

  • AirBnb - $11,899.38 ($3,400/night x 3 nights + cleaning/service/occupancy fees + taxes)
  • Shirts - $500 (est, $12/person * 40 people)
  • Food + consumables - $2500 (est)

That totals around $15k. Grandpa covered 10k of that for us. The remaining 5k is being split among the adults attending. $5k/35 = $142, which we've rounded up to $150 to give us a buffer.

The plan with respect to food is still a little bit up in the the air, at least in that we don't have grocery lists to share (yet). The current budget allots ~$70 per person for the whole trip, which ends up being around ~$15-20/person/day.

The current plan is to go to costco on the way up, and buy a lot of food/drink that should sustain us the entire trip. We expect that this will cover breakfast/lunch/snacks + at least one dinner. Hopefully that would cover all food, but we haven't created an itemized costco shopping list yet so we can't say for certain.

A few people have mentioned trying to get one night catered, which would likely impact what we eat for other meals, but would be really nice for those of us who will be cooking and cleaning the rest of the time. That being said, we haven't done any significant research into it yet and have no idea what our options would be or how much it would cost. If it turns out that we significantly overbudgeted for food, we'll individually return the difference. Otherwise, we'll plan on edging on the side of overbuying and everyone will have the option to take the remaining food home with them.

Also, $2500 of food isn't going to fit into our tiny hatchback, so we will be creating an itemized grocery list and asking for help in buying/transporting food/drink.
